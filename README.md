# Energy-Efficient Wireless Communication Systems (MATLAB-Based Analysis)

**Description:**  
This project investigates energy-efficient techniques in wireless communication systems, with a focus on reducing power consumption in base transceiver stations (BTS) while maintaining Quality of Service (QoS). The work combines analytical modeling and MATLAB-based simulations to study energy-performance trade-offs in modern cellular networks.

## System Overview

The project develops mathematical models to evaluate energy consumption in wireless access networks, particularly at the base station level. It analyzes how factors such as traffic load, operational states (active/idle), and network architecture (macrocell, microcell, picocell, femtocell) influence overall power consumption.

Key aspects include:
- Traffic-aware energy consumption modeling  
- Base station active and idle state analysis  
- Handover behavior under varying traffic conditions  
- Power amplifier efficiency and its impact on system performance  
- Comparative analysis of different cell deployment strategies  

## Simulation & Analysis

MATLAB was used to simulate:
- Energy consumption vs traffic load  
- Number of handovers vs traffic load  
- Power consumption vs amplifier efficiency  
- Efficiency comparison across microcells, picocells, and femtocells  

The results demonstrate that adaptive techniques such as sleep modes and small-cell deployment significantly improve energy efficiency in wireless networks.
